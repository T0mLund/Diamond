## 1. What v2.0 is

vNext+2 is a **research-and-consolidation iteration**, not a build iteration. It does not propose new product surfaces. It does propose:

- A **canonical reframe** of one existing structural object (the bipyramid).
- A **shared frame** for three research threads inherited from vNext+1.
- An **editorial discipline** (the exactness rule) for how geometric claims are made.
- A **governance discipline** (the epistemic-class triage rule) for how every substantive claim is tagged: derived, stipulated, or borrowed.
- A **mixed-reading thesis** that resolves the 26-subzone label question without abandoning the labels.
- A **nested-horizon time-axis** that closes the framework's largest semantic gap.
- A **UX-as-intervention** position on what the model trains when a user inhabits it.
- **Two infrastructure-hygiene deliverables** that close debts incurred during vNext+1's deployment.

The character of vNext+2 is consolidating: it sharpens what the model already says before adding new commitments.

## 2. What vNext+2 is not

vNext+2 is **not** a schema migration. No migration 0010 is written, planned, or pre-committed in vNext+2. The R1 thread may eventually justify one, but vNext+2 does not assume that outcome.

vNext+2 is **not** an answer to the R1, R2, R3 questions. It is a structured frame for *approaching* them. The threads remain open.

vNext+2 is **not** a relaxation of the §4.5 scope guards (per-subject, static positions). Those remain in force. R3 explores how they might one day be relaxed; vNext+2 does not relax them.

## 4. The threads of vNext+2

### 4.1 Containment as organising question

The three reserved research threads from vNext+1 — R1 (interstitial), R2 (M3 super-structure), R3 (dynamic positions) — share a single underlying form: **all three are containment questions at different scales**. vNext+2 makes that observation explicit and uses it to give the three threads a shared vocabulary. See `Diamond_vNext+2_Containment_Note.md`.

This is the substantive editorial move of vNext+2.

### 4.2 The bipyramid as convex hull (canonical reframe)

The anisotropic bipyramid \(|x|+|y|+|z|/\varphi \le 1\) is **identically** the convex hull of the six landmark coordinates (Heaven, Hell, Past, Future on Shell 3; Awakening, Obliteration on Shell 4). vNext+2 adopts the convex-hull description as the **canonical** description in prose; the L¹ inequality remains the computational form.

This is not a change to the geometry. It is a change to how the geometry is described. It makes the bipyramid's structural role (it picks out the six landmarks; nothing else picks them out) immediately legible.

### 4.3 The exactness rule (new editorial discipline)

When a geometric quantity is analytically computable, compute it analytically. When it is not, measure it with declared precision (sampling method + confidence interval) and date the measurement. See Spec vNext+2 §4.6.

The rule was forced by two errors of the same shape in the vNext+1 session — approximations accepted in place of available exact values. It is codified here so the pattern does not repeat.

### 4.4 Measured-geometry table for the four zones (extension of R1)

vNext+1 left the four-zone interior/exterior split partially open. vNext+2 brings the table to its current state:

- Zone 1: **100% inside the bipyramid** (proven analytically).
- Zone 2: **100% inside the bipyramid** (proven analytically).
- Zone 3: **32.21% inside / 67.79% outside** (measured, MC + grid, 2026-05-11).
- Zone 4: **split, percentages not yet computed** (explicit open R1 deliverable).

The Zone 4 row is the only remaining unknown in the table. vNext+2 makes its computation a tracked deliverable; it does not assume an answer.

### 4.5 Council integration (new in revision 2026-05-12)

Three language models (Claude Opus 4.7 Thinking, GPT-5.5 Thinking, Gemini 3.1 Pro Thinking) reviewed the vNext+1 framework. The review converged on three structural moves, all of which are now committed in vNext+2:

- **Spec §4.7 — Epistemic-class triage as governance rule.** Every substantive claim is tagged as *derived* (from another claim already in the framework), *stipulated* (a posited starting point or boundary), or *borrowed* (carried in from an external source with citation). Silent stipulation is prohibited. (H11.) *Origin: Claude Opus 4.7 Thinking, identified the triage move as the strongest rigour gain available without re-architecting.*
- **Spec §6.24 — Mixed reading of sub-zone and landmark labels.** Three classes — T (tag/coordinate-only, default), H (hybrid, the six landmarks plus inherited terms like BEING), S (phenomenological state, empty by default; promotion requires §4.7 warrant). The compositionality problem with the 26-subzone names dissolves because Class T labels do not claim to be states. (H12.) *Origin: GPT-5.5 Thinking, named compositional naming as the largest remaining semantic risk; the mixed reading is a smaller intervention than the wholesale reframe the reviewer suggested, but — confirmed by the user — honest to the framework's actual usage.*
- **Spec §6.7 — Time axis as nested horizons.** Phenomenological Past/Future at inner zones; biographical-cultural at Z2; civilisational/cosmic at Z3; cosmological (Big Bang past pole; heat-death–Big-Rip–CCC future pole) as an **overlay** at Shell 5. The cosmological reading is identification-by-overlay, not identification-by-equation, preserving the per-subject scope guard. The verb "intensifies" is retired for the time axis in favour of *unfolds*, *dilates*, *deepens*. (H13.) *Origin: unanimous across all three reviewers — the time axis was the framework's largest open gap.*

Additionally, the Containment Note grows an appendix **"User as Inhabitant"** (Gemini 3.1 Pro Thinking finding, refined with the user's specification): the camera position in any 3D presentation of the Diamond is itself an epistemic commitment under §4.7. vNext+2 commits to a **two-perspective camera regime** whose transition is keyed to Shell 1 (\(r = \varphi^{-2}\)):

- **Outside Z1 — 3rd-person orbital.** The camera orbits BEING with BEING as the fixed focal point on screen. User controls angular position and zoom. BEING is non-passable; it acts as a *locus*. This is the structural / diagrammatic reading.
- **Inside Z1 — 1st-person light-cone through the aperture.** The viewer (cone apex) is located *behind* BEING. BEING is now an *aperture*: the viewer's perspective passes *through* BEING into the model. User controls viewer orientation and depth behind the aperture. **Depth behind the aperture determines cone width** — close to the aperture, cone widens (open / diffuse awareness); far from the aperture, cone narrows (focused / discriminating attention). The cone extends through whatever radial distances it covers up to Shell 5. Model-side focal depth (depth-of-field) is a deferred future UX consideration, not a vNext+2 commitment.

The transition is keyed to the framework's own geometry, not a UI toggle. BEING plays two roles — *locus* in 3rd-person, *aperture* in 1st-person — and the user only encounters both by passing between modes. This is the framework's first piece of UX that *encodes* a doctrinal commitment (the simultaneous centre-and-emptiness reading of BEING) rather than merely *displays* the model. Two open questions are explicitly carried: the panoramic limit of the wide cone as depth → 0 (180° vs. >180° vs. full sphere), and whether BEING is a non-passable singularity or a passable focal point inside Z1.

### 4.6 Infrastructure hygiene (Phase 1.5b and Phase 1.5c)

Two infrastructure debts surfaced during vNext+1's Phase 1.5 deployment of migration 0009 and are folded into vNext+2 as deployment threads:

- **Phase 1.5b — Idempotent migrations.** Migrations 0001–0008 use `create table` without `if not exists`, so the `db-push` CI workflow fails on every PR merge. The fix is a small PR converting all earlier migrations to idempotent form. Estimated effort: 30 minutes. Unblocks `db-push` so future migrations land automatically.
- **Phase 1.5c — Row-level security audit.** Supabase advisor flagged 39 tables with RLS disabled, meaning the anon key can read or modify every row. Enabling RLS without policies will break Directus access, so this needs paired policy work. vNext+2 scopes the audit; the policy work itself may be larger than one phase.

## 5. Exit criteria

vNext+2 is *complete* when:

1. The Containment Note has been read, accepted, or revised, **including its "User as Inhabitant" appendix.** (Editorial gate.)
2. The bipyramid-as-convex-hull reframe has been carried into Spec §6.22 and into any prose seeded into the v3.1 site or the future Phase 3 site that references the bipyramid.
3. The exactness rule (§4.3) has been applied at least once retroactively — i.e., at least one prior approximation has been replaced by an exact value or a dated measurement, with the prior value visibly removed (not just superseded). This already happened once with the "≈88% of the φ-ball" claim; counts.
4. The Zone 4 volumetric split has either been computed or has a tracked owner and target date.
5. Phase 1.5b (idempotent migrations PR) has been merged and verified by a `db-push` run that completes cleanly. Phase 1.5c (RLS audit) has a written audit document; remediation may extend beyond vNext+2.
6. **The triage rule (§4.7) has been applied at least once to a substantive paragraph of prose** — i.e., the inline-tag, section-tag, or schema-column form has been exercised on real content, not only declared in the Spec. (Discipline gate.)
7. **The mixed-reading thesis (§6.24) has been tested on at least one sub-zone prose draft** — the Roadmap I.5 question has at least a preliminary answer.
8. **The time-axis nested-horizon reading (§6.7) has been used in at least one piece of zone prose at Z3 or Shell 5** — the Roadmap I.6 question has at least a preliminary answer.

vNext+2 is *not* gated on R1, R2, or R3 reaching a decision. Those threads are deliberately open. The frame is what vNext+2 commits to; the answers stay deferred.

## 6. Posture and pacing

vNext+2 is the slow iteration. After the vNext+1 deployment push (Phase 1 → Phase 1.5 done in one evening), the model needs a period of *consolidation rather than expansion*. The four threads above can be approached in any order and at any pace; none of them blocks the others.

Two practical signals that vNext+2 is being approached well:

- Prose that references the bipyramid uses the convex-hull description first and the L¹ inequality as a footnote. (Editorial sign.)
- Any new geometric figure cited in any document is either analytically derived (in which case the derivation is shown) or measured with a method+precision footnote. (Discipline sign.)

When those become habits, vNext+2's editorial work is done.
