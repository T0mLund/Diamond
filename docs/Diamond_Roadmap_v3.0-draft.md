## Purpose

The v3.0 Roadmap is a **deployment-directed research roadmap with an
infrastructure tail closed**. It enumerates:

- Open questions and their decision points (Part I).
- The three reserved research threads (Part II), now operating under
  the shared containment frame.
- Infrastructure history — Phase 1.5b and Phase 1.5c, both CLOSED
  (Part III).
- **The deployment ladder** (Part IV) — Stage 1 ships the geometric
  object; Stages 2 – 4 introduce UX modalities one at a time.
- Decision points and ordering (Part V).

The Roadmap is **non-blocking** for the research threads (R1 / R2 / R3
can be picked up in any order and at any pace), and **strictly
sequential** for the deployment ladder (each stage depends on its
predecessor being live and stable).

## Part I — Cross-cutting research questions

These questions cut across R1, R2, R3 and the editorial work. Each is
enumerated separately so it can be reasoned about independently of any
one thread.

### I.1 — Is containment the right organising question for the next iteration?

The Containment Note proposes that R1, R2, R3 share a single underlying
form. This is itself a hypothesis about the structure of the open
questions, not about the Diamond's geometry or metaphysics.

**Test for "yes":** Approaches to R1 that use the container–inhabitant
vocabulary produce sharper questions than approaches that don't.

**Test for "no":** R1 yields a clean answer using purely geometric
vocabulary; R2 and R3 turn out to be about *different* things (e.g.,
representation rather than containment).

**When to ask:** As soon as any R-thread starts to be worked seriously.
The answer to I.1 should fall out of the first thread engaged.

### I.2 — Should "progression" remain umbrella-only, or should one mechanism reading be elevated?

Spec §6.13 holds five mechanism readings of progression in suspension.
v3.0 carries this forward. The latent question: is suspension a
*permanent* posture for the Diamond, or a temporary one until evidence
picks out a mechanism?

**When to ask:** When the first substantial body of zone prose has been
written. Authors will be forced to pick a reading implicitly; the
implicit choice is data.

### I.3 — What is the relationship between containment (v2-draft cycle) and progression (v1.0)?

These are the two structural questions the model carries forward
unresolved. The Containment Note §6 notes they are not necessarily the
same question. Are they orthogonal? Coupled? Reducible to one another?

**When to ask:** Once both I.1 and I.2 have at least preliminary
answers. Not before.

### I.4 — Does the convex-hull reframe make the bipyramid easier or harder to reason about in prose?

Empirical question, answered by writing. The reframe is committed in
Spec §6.22; this question tracks whether the commitment was a good move.

**When to ask:** After any future zone-prose chunk that references the
bipyramid is drafted. Compare to v1.0 drafts that used the L¹ form.

### I.5 — Does the Class T / Class H / Class S reframe (Spec §6.24) make sub-zone prose flow better?

The mixed-reading thesis is committed in Spec §6.24. But the test is
empirical: when an author writes prose about, say, the *Hell–Past–
Awakening* sub-zone, does treating the label as Class T (default)
produce text that reads more clearly than treating it as a
phenomenological state (Class S) would have?

**Test for "yes":** Class T sub-zone prose reads as *coordinate
description* without losing meaning, and a reader does not protest "but
what *is* that?".

**Test for "no":** Authors find themselves repeatedly smuggling Class S
phenomenological content back in to make the prose live.

**When to ask:** After the first 3 – 5 sub-zone prose chunks are
drafted.

### I.6 — Does the cosmological time-axis overlay (Spec §6.7, Shell 5) interfere with the per-subject scope guard (§4.5)?

The nested-horizon time axis places phenomenological Past / Future at
inner zones and Big Bang / heat-death poles at Shell 5. Shell 5 is, by
other framework commitments, a *cosmic* or *containing* layer, not a
per-subject layer.

The per-subject scope guard (§4.5) says each Diamond is one subject's.
Does the cosmological overlay at Shell 5 quietly violate this?

**Test for "compatible":** The overlay reads naturally as "this subject's
outer horizon of imaginable time, which happens to coincide with what
cosmology calls the Big Bang / heat-death pole".

**Test for "in tension":** Authors keep slipping into "the Big Bang is
at Shell 5" rather than "this subject's outermost past horizon is at
Shell 5".

**When to ask:** Once any zone-prose chunk treats Shell 5 in time terms.
Closely coupled to I.1 and to R3.

### I.7 — What does the framework train when the user inhabits it? *(carries the Containment Note A.8 questions)*

The Containment Note Appendix A commits v3.0 to a two-perspective camera
regime keyed to Shell 1 (\(r = \varphi^{-2}\)): orbital camera centred
on BEING (BEING-as-locus, non-passable) outside Z1, and a 1st-person
light-cone *through* BEING (BEING-as-aperture, viewer behind the
aperture, cone width as a continuous function of depth behind the
aperture) inside Z1.

I.7 tracks whether that commitment holds up as the framework gains more
visual surfaces, and specifically:

- Does the Shell-1 perspective transition *feel* like the framework's
  geometry deciding, rather than like a UI glitch?
- **How panoramic does the 1st-person cone become at small depth?**
- Is BEING a non-passable singularity or a passable focal point inside
  Z1?
- What is the phenomenology of \(r = 0\) — stable terminal, momentary
  threshold, or something else?
- Does the cone-mode view of Shell 5 communicate the cosmological-
  horizon reading of §6.7, or does it confuse?
- Does the *depth-behind-aperture ↔ cone-width* mapping read naturally
  to users who haven't been told the mapping in text? If yes, the
  framework's claim about the phenomenology of attention is being
  *enacted* rather than asserted, which is the strongest possible form
  of the commitment.
- **Future:** Should a model-side focal depth (depth-of-field, distinct
  from depth-behind-aperture) be introduced as a second 1st-person
  control? Deferred per Containment Note §A.7.

**When to ask:** Before any new visual surface ships. The Stage 2
deployment (cone-mode rollout) is the natural occasion for these
questions to acquire data.

### I.8 — Is the Diamond's spatial syntax linear-perspective, scattered-perspective, or mixed? *(parallels §6.24 mixed-reading)*

**Origin.** Spec §6.25 surveys four historical traditions of spatial
representation. Two of them — Western linear perspective and Chinese
*San Yuan* — disagree fundamentally on whether the viewer of a
representation occupies *one* station-point or *multiple kinds of
looking* that can coexist in a single composition. Linear-perspective
demands one; *San Yuan* permits scattering.

**The structural parallel.** I.8 asks of viewpoints what §6.24 asks of
labels: must the framework commit to a single station-point posture
(linear-perspective), or can it compose multiple kinds-of-looking
(scattered-perspective) within a single composition — e.g., a Three.js
scene that supports both orbital and cone simultaneously through
picture-in-picture, or a PDF walkthrough that interleaves *gao yuan /
shen yuan / ping yuan* views of the same model?

**Open sub-questions.**

- **Are orbital and cone simultaneously inhabitable?** Containment Note
  A.3 says no for the *live-camera* experience — they are gated by the
  Shell-1 threshold. But a *composed* surface (a PDF, an essay, an
  explainer scene) can show both at once. Does this break A.3, or does
  A.3 govern only the live-camera experience while composed surfaces
  are free to scatter?
- **Multiple kinds-of-looking within cone-mode.**
- **The container/inhabitant duality of §2.4.**
- **Editorial discipline.** If the framework adopts scattered-
  perspective as the default for composed surfaces, the
  borrowing-vs-stipulation accounting in §6.25.5 needs an entry for
  *the act of scattering itself*.

**When to ask:** Before the first composed surface ships — specifically,
before any Three.js scene that contemplates dual or multi-view rendering,
and before any PDF or essay that mixes orbital and cone illustrations
on a single page. **Stage 2 of the deployment ladder will force the
question.**

### I.9 — Color in spatial representation *(LOCKED architecture in v3.0; sub-questions open)*

**Origin.** The perspective-and-cartography research surfaced a gap:
the four traditions surveyed in Spec §6.25 each had *some* role for
color, but the Diamond had no formal commitment to how color signifies
in its visual surfaces.

**Architecture locked in v3.0 (committed):**

- The three Diamond axes decompose into OKLab as follows:
  - **X axis → chroma** (chroma magnitude).
  - **Y axis → hue** (hue angle).
  - **Z axis → lightness** (L\*).
- This decomposition is a §4.7 **stipulation** and is Class H (it does
  coordinate work and state-name work simultaneously). Documented in
  the Spec.
- The bipyramid renders as a **translucent skin** (the convex hull of
  the six landmarks; §6.22).
- The system supports **togglable palette presets**.

**Open in v3.0 (sub-questions tracked here):**

- **Default palettes.** Starting candidates from the v2 cycle:
  - Y axis: green ↔ yellow.
  - Z axis: blue ↔ red.
  - X axis: chroma magnitude scaled to the axis.
  These are starting points; final palette selection is a tracked UX
  decision, not a v3.0 commitment.
- **Stage at which color ships.** Stage 4 of the deployment ladder
  (Part IV). Earlier stages render the model in a neutral, unpaletted
  scheme (lit translucent geometry; no axis-coded color).
- **Accessibility.** Color-blind-safe alternatives must be one of the
  palette presets. This is a soft commitment; it becomes a hard one
  before Stage 4 ships.
- **Interaction with active-zone fade.** Active-zone fade (I.10 AZ.5)
  works on opacity; color signals overlay. The two must compose
  cleanly. Tracked.

**When to ask the sub-questions:** Before Stage 4 ships (palette
selection, accessibility, interaction with active-zone fade).

### I.10 — Active-zone semantics *(LOCKED architecture in v3.0; sub-questions open)*

**Origin.** The v2-draft cycle surfaced the question of how the
framework's "active zone" — the zone the user is currently engaged with
— is selected, displayed, and disambiguated.

**Architecture locked in v3.0 (committed):**

- **AZ.1 — Cone-front intersection chooses the active zone.** The
  active zone is the zone the user's cone is currently pointing into
  (in cone-mode) or the zone the camera's reticle is on (in orbital
  mode). The selection is geometric, not modal.
- **AZ.5 — Continuous opacity fade default.** The active zone renders
  at full opacity; non-active zones fade smoothly to a lower opacity.
  Discrete switching is available as an **opt-in preset**, not the
  default.

**Open in v3.0 (sub-questions tracked here):**

- **AZ.2 — Radial vs bipyramid layers.** Does the active-zone selection
  respect *radial layers* (Z1 / Z2 / Z3 / Z4) or the *bipyramid
  partition* (inside-B vs outside-B)? Or a hybrid (e.g., inside-B
  inherits the radial active zone; outside-B layers separately)?
- **AZ.3 — Shell-1 interaction.** How does active-zone selection
  interact with the Shell-1 perspective transition (Containment Note
  §A.3.3)? Specifically: in cone-mode (inside Z1), is "the zone Z1 is
  in" trivially Z1, or does the cone reach out through BEING and select
  an outer zone?
- **AZ.4 — Shell-5 / Z5-overlay policy.** Shell 5 has no zone proper
  (it is the outer horizon, NULL-zoned). What is the active-zone
  selection's behaviour when the cone or reticle points beyond Shell 4?
- **AZ.6 — "Behind" disambiguation.** When the cone passes through the
  active zone and into another, which zone wins? Possibilities: nearest
  along the cone axis; furthest; deepest by reticle; user-toggled.
- **AZ.7 — Modal vs ontological.** Is the active-zone selection a
  *modal* surface (the user asks for it) or an *ontological* feature
  (the framework always has one active zone)?

**When to ask the sub-questions:** Before Stage 3 ships (active-zone
fade rollout). AZ.2 and AZ.3 are gating questions for Stage 3; AZ.4,
AZ.6, AZ.7 can be settled during or shortly after Stage 3.

## Part II — The three reserved threads (under containment frame)

### R1 — Interstitial geometry and zone partition revision

**Frame:** Is the bipyramid a *container* (zone-defining) or an
*inhabitant* (landmark)?

**Current status (v3.0):**

- Bipyramid is documented as an **inhabitant** in Spec §6.22.4.
- Three-row evidence in the measured-geometry table (Spec §6.23):
  Zone 1 (100% inside, analytic; H9 theorem), Zone 2 (100% inside,
  analytic; H9 theorem), Zone 3 (32.21% / 67.79%, measured 2026-05-11).
- Zone 4 row is open.

**Deliverables (in priority order):**

1. **R1.1 — Compute the Zone 4 volumetric split.** Same method as
   Zone 3 (MC + grid cross-check). Estimated effort: ~10 minutes of
   compute, ~30 minutes to write up. Output: a single row added to
   Spec §6.23 and to the Containment Note Appendix.
   **This is the only quantitative gap remaining for R1.**
   Owner: open. Target: before any R1 decision discussion.

2. **R1.2 — Container-vs-inhabitant decision framework.** A short
   document (~3 pages) listing the consequences of each route:
   - Route A: keep bipyramid as inhabitant; consequences for zone
     prose, sub-zone naming, item anchoring.
   - Route B: promote bipyramid to zone-defining surface; consequences
     for a hypothetical `migration 0015`, sub-zone re-enumeration,
     Directus collection updates, anchor schema.
   - Route C: change the bipyramid to a regular octahedron of
     circumradius φ (the "Zone 3 cleanly bisected" reading); consequences
     for landmark coordinates (Heaven / Hell / Past / Future shift to
     Shell 4), Spec §6.22 rewrite, all prior renders.
   Owner: open. Target: when I.1 is answered "yes".

3. **R1.3 — Trial prose passage.** Write one or two paragraphs of
   Zone 3 zone-prose under each of Routes A and B. The author's
   experience writing each is data for R1.2. Output: comparison memo.
   Owner: Vegar or collaborator. Target: after R1.2.

**Out of scope for R1 in v3.0:** Migration `0015` SQL or any later
migration. No migration is written until at least Routes A vs B is
resolved.

### R2 — M3 super-structure for multiple Diamonds

**Frame:** What structure *contains* a community of per-subject
Diamonds?

**Current status:** Carried over from v1.0 §6.21 unchanged. M3 remains
a placeholder name. No new geometric or schema commitments in v3.0.

**Deliverables (low priority for v3.0; this thread is the slowest):**

1. **R2.1 — Survey of candidate containment topologies.** A short
   document listing options:
   - Manifold (Diamonds as points)
   - Fibre bundle (Diamonds as fibres over a shared base)
   - Lattice of vantage points (Diamonds as mutual projections)
   - Other / placeholder
   Each option gets one paragraph. Owner: open. Target: no urgency.

2. **R2.2 — Identification criterion.** Under any candidate topology,
   *what makes a shape seen differently from two vantage points the
   same shape?* Owner: open. Target: after R2.1.

**Out of scope:** Any commitment to a particular M3 structure. R2 in
v3.0 stays at the survey level.

### R3 — Dynamic positions and animated trajectories

**Frame:** What structure *contains* one subject's trajectory through
their Diamond over time?

**Current status:** Schema affordance already exists — `items.effective_period`
columns (added in migration 0009) allow multiple rows per item with
non-overlapping periods. The schema does not, however, commit to *what
those rows mean*.

**Deliverables:**

1. **R3.1 — Editorial policy for `effective_period`.** A short policy
   doc answering: under what circumstances should an item have multiple
   rows with different `effective_period`s? Examples: a person before
   and after an awakening event; a concept that shifts position as a
   tradition evolves; a practice that migrates between zones. The
   policy should be **conservative** — only allow multi-row items when
   the position change is documented and provenanced. Owner: open.
   Target: before any item with multiple effective_period rows is
   inserted via Directus or any other surface.

2. **R3.2 — Trajectory data model question.** Is a trajectory:
   - A sequence of static items (current schema can express this with
     multiple rows)
   - A single item with a time-parameterised position field (would
     require schema change)
   - A relation-graph object (could be expressed in `item_relations`
     with a new `transitions_to` relation kind, no schema change)
   This is **not** a question to settle in v3.0. R3.2 is a tracked
   question for v4.0 or later. Listed here only to flag that R3.1 is
   the only R3 deliverable in v3.0 scope.

**Out of scope:** Animation. Rendering of time evolution. The "subject
as moving point" abstraction. All deferred beyond v3.0.

## Part III — Infrastructure history (Phase 1.5b and Phase 1.5c — CLOSED)

Both Phase 1.5b and Phase 1.5c are **closed** in v3.0. This Part records
the history.

### Phase 1.5b — Idempotent migrations (CLOSED)

**Original problem (v2-draft scope):** Migrations 0001 – 0008 used
`create table` without `if not exists`, so the `db-push` CI workflow
failed on every PR merge.

**Remediation shipped on `main`:** Migrations 0001 – 0008 were replaced
by a single idempotent baseline snapshot — `0001_baseline.sql` — that
captures the full schema state at the time of the cut-over. The
baseline is idempotent (every `create` guarded by `if not exists`;
functions use `create or replace`; triggers drop-then-create).

**Verification:** CI `db-push` workflow runs to completion on `main`.

**Status:** CLOSED. Merged in PR #5 (combined Phase 1.5b + 1.5c PR,
2026-05-16). Cross-reference: [`main` history](https://github.com/T0mLund/Diamond/commits/main).

### Phase 1.5c — Row-level security remediation (CLOSED)

**Original problem (v2-draft scope):** Supabase advisor flagged 39
tables in the `public` schema with RLS disabled. The anon key could
read and modify every row.

**Remediation shipped on `main` (5 migrations):**

- `0010_phase_1_5c_rls_remediation_phase_a.sql` — enable RLS on geometry
  tables.
- `0011_phase_1_5c_rls_remediation_phase_a2.sql` — enable RLS on items
  and item_relations.
- `0012_phase_1_5c_rls_remediation_phase_b.sql` — enable RLS on content
  and Directus housekeeping tables.
- `0013_phase_1_5c_rls_remediation_phase_d1.sql` — function hardening
  (`search_path = ''`).
- `0014_phase_1_5c_rls_remediation_phase_d2.sql` — view hardening
  (`security_invoker = true`).

**Posture committed:** **Fully-private.** All RLS-enabled tables deny
anon access. Read access is granted only via service-role credentials
held by the project owner and the assisting agent. This is consistent
with the user's standing directive:

> *Now: Only you and I can write to the project. Others can only read.
> Later: Others are invited in as collaborators... This is 6-12 months
> away from now and not important.*

The "others can only read" provision is interpreted *strictly* at v3.0:
no anonymous read surface is enabled at the database. Public read at
the *application* layer (Stage 1 + of the deployment ladder) is supplied
through a service-role-credentialed read path, not through anon Supabase
queries.

**Verification:** Supabase advisor flags cleared. CI `db-push` green on
`main`. Cross-reference: [`main` history](https://github.com/T0mLund/Diamond/commits/main).

**Status:** CLOSED.

### What Phase 1.5b / 1.5c do *not* settle

- **Collaboration model.** R3 of the original Phase 1.5c plan (audit-
  classify-roll-out) is in effect *foreclosed by* the fully-private
  posture: no anon access means no further per-table classification is
  required for the present iteration. If the user later opens the
  project to collaborators, the classification work resumes.
- **Public read posture.** Currently *not enabled*. If Stage 1
  deployment is publicly linked, the application's read path needs an
  edge function or equivalent to surface schema data without granting
  anon DB access.

## Part IV — Deployment ladder *(NEW in v3.0)*

This Part is the substantive new content of v3.0. Deployment is staged.
Each stage ships only what the previous stage has stabilised.

### IV.0 — Standing user directives that govern the ladder

Carried forward verbatim:

> *Objective 1 is to achieve a functioning model. Objective 2 is
> security. Always look for ways to simplify and avoid unnecessary
> confusion.*

> *This is not urgent. The project is not deployed and no public links
> have been made available. Proceed cautiously.*

> *The technical details are beyond my reach, so I have to give high
> level instruction and trust your implementation.*

> *Keep these options open as much as possible. The exact need is not
> yet determined.* (re: WYSIWYG)

> *Now: Only you and I can write to the project. Others can only read.
> Later: Others are invited in as collaborators... This is 6-12 months
> away from now and not important.*

These directives govern every stage. No stage publishes a public link
without explicit user approval. No stage opens write access beyond the
project owner and the assisting agent without explicit user approval.

### IV.1 — Stage 1: Geometric object (first shippable build)

**Scope:**

- Shells 1 – 4 rendered as translucent spheres in a Three.js scene.
- The three axes (X / Y / Z) rendered as numerical reference lines.
  No axis labels beyond X / Y / Z; semantic content lives in zones and
  landmarks (Spec §2).
- **Bipyramid skin** rendered as a translucent surface (convex hull of
  the six landmarks; Spec §6.22 canonical form).
- **Seven Class-H landmarks** rendered as labelled points:
  Heaven \((1,0,0)\), Hell \((-1,0,0)\), Future \((0,1,0)\),
  Past \((0,-1,0)\), Obliteration \((0,0,\varphi)\),
  Awakening \((0,0,-\varphi)\), BEING \((0,0,0)\). No other items, no
  other labels.
- **Orbital camera only.** 3rd-person mode per Containment Note §A.3.1.
  BEING is non-passable.
- **Full-frame web app.** No chrome; the diamond fills the viewport.
- **Read-only for everyone but the project owner and the assisting
  agent.** No edit surfaces.
- Neutral color rendering: no axis-coded palette. Lit translucent
  geometry; the user can see what is in front and what is behind, but
  no color signals axis identity. Stage 4 introduces color.
- **No public link.** Stage 1 ships behind whatever surface the user
  approves (private Netlify preview, local-only run, password-gated
  preview). The default is the most-restrictive option compatible with
  "the user can see it on a device of their choice."

**Acceptance criteria for Stage 1 done:**

1. The Three.js scene renders correctly on a recent Chrome / Firefox
   build at a standard desktop resolution.
2. All seven landmarks are present, labelled, and at the correct
   coordinates (verified against Spec §3.1 — the exactness rule applies
   here; coordinates must be the φ-exact values, not floating-point
   approximations rounded for prettiness).
3. The bipyramid skin is the convex hull of the six landmarks
   (not the L¹ rendering of a generic shape; the implementation should
   construct the hull from the landmark coordinates so any future
   landmark migration deforms the skin correctly).
4. Orbital camera controls (angle + zoom) feel responsive and behave
   sensibly at the inner zoom limit (the camera should *not* slip into
   Z1 in Stage 1 — Stage 2's threshold logic is not yet in place).
5. The build is deployable to Netlify (or equivalent) with the
   service-role read path; no anon DB access; no public link unless
   the user explicitly opens one.

**Dependencies:** None of the open R-threads block Stage 1. The
Containment Note Appendix A.3.1 is the spec.

**Estimated effort:** Several hours of Three.js work for the assisting
agent. The user's role is high-level review and approval of the
rendered output.

### IV.2 — Stage 2: Cone-mode and the Shell-1 transition

**Scope (adds to Stage 1):**

- **1st-person cone-mode** activated when the orbital camera zooms
  inside Z1, per Containment Note §A.3.2 and §A.3.3.
- **Shell-1 transition** as the keyed perspective-mode switch (not a
  UI toggle).
- **Viewer-behind-aperture** controls: orientation + depth-behind-
  aperture; the latter modulates cone width per the §A.3.2 stipulation
  (close = wide / open awareness; far = narrow / focused attention).
- The cone passes *through* BEING; the viewer is behind it; the model
  is on the other side.
- The *gao yuan / shen yuan / ping yuan* Class H tags from §A.3.2
  supplement are **reserved** as future UX surfaces but not exposed as
  toggles at Stage 2. They live in the documentation.

**Acceptance criteria for Stage 2 done:**

1. Zooming into Z1 in 3rd-person mode triggers the regime flip without
   visible discontinuity in the geometry on screen.
2. Cone-mode controls (orientation + depth) feel responsive.
3. Depth-behind-aperture maps continuously to cone width with no
   discrete steps.
4. Retreating from cone-mode back past Shell 1 returns the user to
   orbital mode at a sensible camera position.
5. The cone-width-as-attention stipulation (§A.3.2) is visibly
   labelled in the UX (either as a one-line caption, a tooltip on a
   help button, or equivalent). The framework's commitment to declaring
   its stipulations applies to the UX as well as the documents.

**Dependencies:** Stage 1 stabilised. I.7 sub-questions about the
panoramic limit and BEING-as-singularity-vs-passable are surfaced for
the first time at Stage 2 but do not block it; they are *tested* at
Stage 2 and resolved or carried forward.

### IV.3 — Stage 3: Active-zone fade

**Scope (adds to Stage 2):**

- **Continuous opacity fade** of the active zone vs the rest
  (AZ.5 default).
- **Cone-front intersection** chooses the active zone (AZ.1).
- The opt-in preset for **discrete switching** is implemented but off
  by default.

**Acceptance criteria for Stage 3 done:**

1. As the user moves the camera (in orbital mode) or rotates the cone
   (in cone-mode), the active zone changes smoothly. No abrupt
   transitions.
2. Active-zone-fade composes cleanly with cone-mode's depth-behind-
   aperture control (both modulate opacity; the composition is
   well-defined and visually legible).
3. The user can toggle the discrete-switching preset on and off.
4. Behaviour at Shell-5 / Z5-overlay (AZ.4) is well-defined — either
   "no active zone" or "stays on whatever zone was last active". The
   policy is documented.
5. AZ.2 (radial vs bipyramid layers) is committed before Stage 3 ships.
   See I.10 sub-questions.

**Dependencies:** Stage 2 stabilised. I.10 sub-questions AZ.2 and AZ.3
must be resolved (committed) before Stage 3 ships.

### IV.4 — Stage 4: Color palettes (OKLab decomposition)

**Scope (adds to Stage 3):**

## Appendix — Deferred to v4.0+ (future-computational threads)

### F.1 — Grid-cell hexagonal tiling vs. bipyramidal symmetries

**Origin.** The perspective-and-cartography research surfaced
[grid-cell research from O'Keefe, Moser, and Moser](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2014/press-release/)
(2014 Nobel Prize) showing that the mammalian entorhinal cortex tiles
spatial environments with a *hexagonal* grid. Mammalian spatial
cognition appears to be hexagonally organised at the neural level. The
Diamond's bipyramid (Spec §6.22) has a 4-fold equatorial symmetry and
is *not* hexagonal.

**The question.** Is there a hexagonal reading of the Diamond that the
bipyramid currently obscures? Specifically:

- **Tiling.** If the Diamond's volume were tiled by hexagonal cells
  rather than by rectilinear sub_zones, would semantic relations
  become more legible? Less? Differently?
- **Symmetry mismatch.** The bipyramid's 4-fold equatorial symmetry
  maps four cardinal landmarks. A 6-fold symmetry would map six. Is
  the commitment to four equatorial landmarks a stipulation that
  could be revisited?
- **Composition.** Hexagonal tilings have a different
  shape-of-neighbourhood than rectilinear tilings.
- **Computational cost.** Hexagonal Voronoi tilings of bipyramidal
  volumes, or hexagonal-grid ↔ rectilinear-grid conversions, are
  computational questions: they need code, profiling, and data.

**Status.** *Deferred to v4.0+ and tagged for future computational /
research effort.*

**Epistemic status, in advance.** Any hexagonal reading is, by default,
a §4.7 *stipulation* unless explicit warrant is found in the framework's
own first principles or in a borrowing from grid-cell research that
respects §4.7's discipline.

**When to revisit.** v4.0 or later — specifically, after Stage 2+ of
the deployment ladder is live (so the cone-mode phenomenology can be
tested against any hexagonal hypothesis), the `items` table has enough
populated content to test adjacency queries, and a separate
decision-point is reached on whether the framework should support
adjacency queries at all.

**Cross-references.** Spec §6.22 (bipyramid), Spec §6.25 (historical
lineage), Roadmap I.8 (scattered perspective), Containment Note §A.9
(UX-as-intervention).
